## Detailed Instrument Profiles

### NICER (Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer)
- **Location:** International Space Station
- **Capability:** X-ray timing of millisecond pulsars
- **Key targets:** PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620
- **SSZ relevance:** Direct NS mass-radius measurement → redshift comparison
- **Precision needed:** Δz/z < 5% to distinguish SSZ from GR

### NANOGrav (North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves)
- **Capability:** Pulsar timing arrays
- **Key data:** 15-year dataset (2023 release)
- **SSZ relevance:** Strong-field timing residuals
- **Precision needed:** Δt < 10% to distinguish

### Next-Generation EHT (ngEHT)
- **Status:** In development
- **Improvement:** 3-10x resolution over current EHT
- **SSZ relevance:** BH shadow diameter measurement
- **Precision needed:** 1.3% shadow size resolution

### GRAVITY/VLTI
- **Location:** Very Large Telescope Interferometer, Paranal, Chile
- **Objects:** S2, S0-2 and other S-stars near Sgr A*
- **SSZ relevance:** Weak-field validation (SSZ = GR)
- **Anti-circularity:** Data not used for SSZ calibration

## Experimental Data Sources

### ESO S-Star Data (Sgr A*)
- GRAVITY Collaboration, ESO
- Perihelion precession, redshift comparison
- Data not used for SSZ calibration (anti-circularity)

### NICER Neutron Star Data
- NASA Goddard Space Flight Center
- Mass-radius determination, redshift predictions
- Prediction: z_SSZ = z_GR + 13% (testable!)

### GPS Time Measurements
- US Naval Observatory, BIPM
- Time difference ground ↔ satellite = ~45.85 μs/day
- Weak-field validation (SSZ ≡ GR)
- Accuracy: < 1 ns/day

### Pound-Rebka Experiment
- Gravitational redshift over 22.6 m height
- Δν/ν = 2.46 × 10⁻¹⁵
- Weak-field validation (SSZ ≡ GR, <0.01% deviation)

### EHT Black-Hole Shadow
- M87* (2019), Sgr A* (2022)
- Shadow diameter measurement
- SSZ prediction: -1.3% smaller than GR

### Gaia Stellar Catalog
- ESA Gaia Mission, DR3 (2022)
- Parallaxes, proper motions, radial velocities
- Stellar parameters for validation

### Schumann Resonance Data
- Various observatories (Hylaty, Nagycenk)
- Fundamental frequency ~7.83 Hz and overtones
- Explorative scaling analysis (ssz-schumann repo)
